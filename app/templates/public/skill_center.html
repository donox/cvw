Skill Center & Mentoring

What Is the Skill Center?

The CVW Skill Center is a member resource offering hands-on lathe time, tool use, and one-on-one mentoring from experienced turners. Whether you are learning for the first time or refining a specific technique, the Skill Center provides a supportive, well-equipped environment.

Sessions

Schedule
Sessions are held on selected Saturdays — see the calendar for dates
Location
CVW Skills Center — contact the Skills Center Director for the address
Who Can Attend
CVW members of all skill levels; guests may attend one session before joining
Cost
Included with membership; small materials fee may apply for some sessions

What to Expect

A typical Skill Center session accommodates a small group of members working on individual projects with guidance available from the Skills Center Director and volunteer mentors. Lathes, basic turning tools, and safety equipment are provided. Participants are encouraged to bring their own tools as they progress.

Topics covered include:

  • Safe lathe operation and tool handling
  • Bowl and spindle turning fundamentals
  • Tool sharpening and maintenance
  • Finishing techniques — oils, lacquers, and waxes
  • Hollowing and advanced forms
  • Member-requested skill topics
